Article 1

During a lively presentation at the local gallery, a thoughtful suggestion was made that art could become a source of unity for the entire community. The gallery was renowned for its superior and superb collections and even held approval from the city's authority. Many found the experience awesome, although one artist—feeling rather vain—chose to sleep on his innovative ideas rather than share them during the presentation. In the midst of a winter that recalled the chill of the Arctic, he worked instead of exhibiting his work. He let creative inspiration surround him like a fine string caught in the dust of time, and afterward he dramatically unsheathed his metaphorical sword against critics—even though he felt ashamed of his earlier hesitation.

Article 3

At a bustling innovation fair, contestants were challenged to invent novel solutions and freely chat about their breakthroughs. The judges, highly sensitive to original ideas, encouraged every participant to subscribe to a vision that was never arbitrary. One display even weighed over a kilo of recycled materials, as some competitors moved in a swift tempo, choosing whichever design best captured the public eye. Yet one participant would often choke under pressure, unable to offer a cogent assessment or explain his concepts clearly. Although he once smoked a cigarette to calm his nerves, it did little to bother his anxiety during an emergency. His poor eyesight for numbers made arithmetic a daunting challenge.
